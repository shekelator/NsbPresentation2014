Low-Friction SOA with NServiceBus

Hi, I'm Dave, and I have a monolithic legacy system

(Hi, Dave)

Wai u no like monolith?

Well, for starters:

  • Everything is coupled, so it's difficult to know what will be impacted by a change
  • System is too complicated to grok

We needed to break it up

  • Good fences make good neighbors
  • You know where the coupling is
  • Smaller pieces can be changed more easily (As long as they fulfill their contract)

Architectural decisions

How do the components communicate?

  • Semantically: "Do this!" vs. "I did this"
  • Technically: HTTP? Sync vs. async?

This is core to SOA in the NServiceBus style:

Service Autonomy

Services are in control of their destiny. Instead of getting bossed around, they observe events and do what is necessary to fulfill the business capabilities for which they are responsible for

Why not RPC?

  • Services should be autonomous
  • Fallacies of Distributed Computing

Why not HTTP?

  • Not durable/transactional
  • Synchronous => tends toward temporal coupling

NServiceBus makes it easy

  • Asynchronous (durable) messaging
  • Bus vs. Broker
  • Point-to-point and publish-subscribe
  • Multiple transport options
    • MSMQ (default)
    • RabbitMQ
    • SQL Server
    • Azure Service Bus/Azure Storage Queues

MOAR EASY THINGS

  • Use of Microsoft DTC makes localized transactions easy
  • Sagas
    • Long-running processes
    • State machine: class that handles multiple events and maintains state
    • Can perform any processing or persistence like a normal handler
    • Useful for out-of-order messages
